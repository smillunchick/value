What the NGM chapter actually proves

Three things that chapter demonstrated — none of which show up on a resume.

  • Transparent communication under pressure

    Your SEC filing letter explaining the restructuring to staff is the kind of leadership communication that builds trust precisely because it doesn't hide behind corporate language. Most CEOs in that position retreat into legal boilerplate. You wrote something human.

  • Pragmatic capital stewardship

    Recognizing that the public markets were not the right structure for NGM's remaining programs and executing a take-private was a decision most CEOs delay until it's too late. You made the call before the math forced your hand.

  • Knowing when to hand off

    Voluntarily departing after the second restructuring rather than presiding over a shell shows judgment that prioritizes the company over personal identity. That's the kind of discipline investors remember.
