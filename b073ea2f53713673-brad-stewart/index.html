02
The Competitive Landscape

Four positioning strategies, one word

Platform Play

MuleSoft / Salesforce

"Agent Fabric" — positioning MCP as the governance layer for all agents, not just Salesforce ones. The story: you already have APIs, we make them agent-ready. Biggest advantage is distribution through the Salesforce install base.

Protocol First

Workato

"Enterprise MCP" is already production-grade, not on a roadmap. They're framing it as "the agentic enterprise" — agents embedded across every system, role, and process. Their pitch: governance, not just connectivity.

Data Trust

Informatica

"CLAIRE Agents" — not selling integration at all. They're selling trusted data as the prerequisite for agents. The angle: your AI is only as good as the metadata underneath it. Different lane entirely.

Sprawl Control

Boomi

"Agent Control Tower" — governing agent sprawl before it spirals. Their positioning: you're already deploying agents everywhere, and nobody has visibility. Boomi does. Pragmatic narrative aimed at ops teams.

Every vendor says "agentic." The differentiation is in which problem they claim to own.

03
The Protocol Shift

MCP is quietly redrawing the competitive map

The Model Context Protocol is becoming to AI agents what REST became to web APIs — the standard way agents discover and interact with enterprise tools. Gartner calls it a "real-time context mesh," and warns that 40% of agentic AI initiatives risk cancellation without one.

  • SnapLogic expanded MCP support in September 2025 — early, but not first. MuleSoft and Workato both have production MCP implementations now.
  • The risk isn't being late to MCP. It's that MCP becomes the conversation, and whichever platform "owns" MCP in the enterprise buyer's mind gets the shortlist spot.
  • Workato is currently the most aggressive at claiming MCP leadership, publishing head-to-head comparisons that position competitors as "on the roadmap" while they're "production-ready."

Sources: MuleSoft Mar 2026, Workato Oct 2025, SnapLogic Sep 2025

04
SnapLogic's Position

What's working and what's blending in

DISTINCTIVE

  • "The Agentic Integration Company" as a tagline is bold — it plants a flag rather than hedging. None of the competitors have claimed "agentic integration" as a category noun this directly.
  • AgentCreator is a real product, not a rebrand. Letting non-developers build enterprise agents on top of existing integration pipelines is a concrete, sellable thing. Version 3.0 shipped April 2025.
  • Gartner Visionary placement in the 2025 Magic Quadrant for Data Integration Tools — external validation that matters in enterprise procurement.

AT RISK OF BLENDING

  • "Agentic Enterprise" language is used by Workato, Boomi, and Salesforce almost interchangeably. When everyone uses the same phrase, it stops meaning anything.
  • The SnapGPT + AgentCreator narrative currently reads as two separate products, not one story. Buyers need to hear a single arc: "your integration layer is your AI layer."
  • The Stanford partnership and academic positioning are strong signals that aren't yet threaded into the commercial narrative. They live on LinkedIn, not on the product pages.
05
Observations

Three things the winners will get right

  • Own a problem, not a product category. Workato owns "governance for agentic systems." MuleSoft owns "making your existing APIs agent-ready." Informatica owns "trusted data for AI." The positioning question for SnapLogic isn't "what do we do" — it's "what keeps the CIO up at night that only we solve?" AgentCreator gives you a real answer here: the bottleneck isn't building agents, it's giving business teams safe access to build agents on enterprise data without breaking things.
  • The word "integration" might be holding the narrative back. Integration implies plumbing. It implies the thing you do before the interesting thing happens. The companies winning this positioning war are reframing integration as intelligence infrastructure — the nervous system, not the pipes. SnapLogic's "agentic integration" tagline is halfway there. The risk is that "integration" anchors buyers to the old mental model.
  • The CEO transition story is a narrative asset nobody else has. Every other iPaaS vendor is led by a career integration or SaaS executive. A CEO who came from aviation, private equity, and fintech — who chose this company — can tell a story about why agentic integration matters to the business in a way that product people can't. That cross-industry credibility is underused.
