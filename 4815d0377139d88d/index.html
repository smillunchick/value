The Narrative Shift

Prepared for Rohit Agarwal · February 21, 2026

Your Series A blog post has one line that tells the whole story:

“Engineering gets reliability. Finance gets accountability. Leadership gets answers.”

That is the right instinct. It is also the beginning of a harder conversation, not the end of one.

The Inflection

The story that won 24,000 developer organizations does not automatically translate into the story that wins a Fortune 500 CFO.

  • 70% of startups fail the narrative test at the Series A → enterprise transition [Confidence: High]
  • 46% of enterprise AI buying decisions now come from business leaders, not technical teams [Confidence: High]
  • 86% track cost reduction. 66% want defined KPIs before signing. [Confidence: High]

The buyers are changing. The narrative needs to change with them.

Three Rooms, One Product

Room 1: Engineering (You Own This)

What they hear: Latency. Uptime. Developer experience. Open source.

Portkey today: 99.9999% uptime, 60+ providers, 6,200+ GitHub stars. “AI that never breaks.”

The risk: Assuming the rest of the organization speaks this language.

Room 2: Finance (They Write the Check)

What they hear: Cost attribution. Budget predictability. Spend accountability.

The translation:

Technical Financial
“500 billion tokens processed daily” “$180M in annualized AI spend under active governance”
“Unified gateway” “Single pane of glass for AI cost across every team”
“Multi-provider routing” “Vendor consolidation and budget control”

86% of enterprise AI buyers track cost reduction as a primary metric. The CFO does not care about tokens. The CFO cares whether the AI budget is visible, attributable, and within policy.

Room 3: Security & Compliance (They Can Kill the Deal)

What they hear: Audit trails. Data residency. Policy enforcement. SOC 2, HIPAA.

The translation:

Technical Compliance
“AI guardrails” “Real-time policy enforcement with full audit trail”
“Gateway logging” “Every AI decision auditable across every model and team”
“Access controls” “Identity-based permissions with budget boundaries”

84% of CXOs say integrated security and compliance are mandatory before approving an AI tool. [Confidence: High]

The Competitive Window

TrueFoundry’s competitive analysis already frames Portkey as “application-scoped, not organization-wide.” [Confidence: High]

This is dangerous not because it is true, but because it could become true in the market’s perception if left unanswered.

Your stated priority of “governance for agentic AI” is a category no one has defined yet. That is a window. Define it before a competitor’s framing solidifies.

One Translation Exercise

Take a single metric through all three rooms:

“120 million daily requests”

  • Engineering: “Battle-tested at scale most AI infrastructure never reaches. Zero downtime.”
  • Finance: “120M AI decisions per day under active cost management. Every request tracked and within budget.”
  • Compliance: “120M auditable AI decisions per day. Every request logged, every policy enforced in real time.”

Same fact. Three framings. Three different reasons to buy.

The Work

The same conviction that built a product 24,000 organizations trust needs to be translated into the language of each room. The conviction stays. The vocabulary shifts.

You wrote “AI that never breaks.” That is not a developer tagline. That is a promise. The question is whether the CFO and the CISO hear it as the same promise the engineering team does.

Right now, they cannot. The translation is the work.
