One Story, Three Rooms

Prepared for Grant Gunnison · February 21, 2026

You just raised $16.8 million. Revenue grew ten times in a year. You are in five states heading to more. You are doubling your team from 25 to 50.

Every one of these facts is a communication challenge disguised as good news.

The Problem

The story that got you here was Grant’s story. Your father’s construction business. MIT. NASA. Coming back to build the tool the trades never had.

That story is powerful. But as Zero Homes scales, it needs to work differently in different rooms.

Companies with successful products have a greater than 80% chance of failure during scaling. Investors attribute 65% of failures to people and organizational issues, not product or market problems. — McKinsey, 2024

Three Rooms, One Story

Room 1: Investors

What they hear: Platform infrastructure. TAM penetration. Unit economics. Moat.

Proof Point Why It Matters
Quote turnaround: 1 day → 30 minutes by EOY 2026 Machine velocity
Revenue: 10x year over year Growth trajectory
5 states and expanding Market penetration
Remote-first digital assessment Structural cost advantage (no truck rolls)

Matt Eggers at Prelude already gave you the frame: “the missing digital infrastructure for home upgrades.” That is the investor narrative. It is about the machine, not the mission.

Room 2: Contractors

What they hear: Reliability. Lead quality. Reduced overhead. Calendars filled.

Proof Point Why It Matters
No lead fees Unlike every competitor marketplace
Automated design, sizing, permitting Overhead elimination
DOE and ACCA certified designs Reduced callback risk

Your construction family background is the trust credential in this room. It says: I grew up watching my father deal with the same overhead you deal with.

“We do the part you hate so you can do the part you are great at.”

Room 3: Homeowners

What they hear: Simplicity. Trust. Savings. No hassle.

Proof Point Why It Matters
20% average savings vs. traditional contractors Cost
No in-home visit for initial assessment Convenience
DOE and ACCA validated designs Trust
Money-back quality guarantee Risk removal

“You want to electrify. We make it easy.”

The Connective Thread

The word that stays constant across all three rooms: friction.

  • For investors, friction reduction = platform efficiency and unit economics
  • For contractors, friction reduction = overhead elimination
  • For homeowners, friction reduction = simplicity and trust

When you are in a room unsure which version to tell, return to friction. It works everywhere.

The Internal Story

As you double from 25 to 50 and expand geographically, the founding narrative needs to be transmissible without you personally telling it.

Three moves:

  1. Write the origin story as an artifact. Two paragraphs every new hire reads in week one. Your father’s business. The overhead killing independent contractors. The decision to leave aerospace and build the fix.

  2. Codify “contractor-first” as a principle. “We don’t charge contractors for leads. We fill their calendars.” That is not a pricing decision. It is a values decision. Make it explicit.

  3. Let the mission carry without the missionary. At 50 people across multiple states, you will not be in every room. The mission needs to live in the onboarding deck, the weekly all-hands, and the way the product team talks about design decisions.

Your Edge

You have something most climate tech founders do not. You grew up in the trades, studied at MIT, worked at NASA, and came back to build software for the people your father worked alongside.

That is a credibility bridge that connects every room in this framework. The challenge is not whether the story is good. It is whether the story scales.
