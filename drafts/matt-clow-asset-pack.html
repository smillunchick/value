High-Stakes Conversation Prep: "What Are You Doing Next?"

For 12 years you walked into rooms as TELUS. The brand preceded you. Now you're walking into rooms as Matt Clow. The credibility has to be personal from the first sentence.

This is a different kind of performance pressure. You know this from football — a QB who moves from a system offense to a new team doesn't forget how to read defenses. But the timing is different. The internal rhythm has to recalibrate.

Before Every Significant Conversation

  1. Know your one sentence. Working draft: "I spent 12 years doing enterprise strategy at TELUS, mostly in Atlantic Canada, and I just left to figure out where I can do the most interesting version of that work independently."
  2. Regulate before you perform. 90 seconds slow exhale breathing. Feet on the floor. You're not auditioning — you're having a conversation about whether there's a fit.
  3. Lead with curiosity, not credentials. Your 12 years shows in the quality of your questions, not in how you describe your resume.
  4. Name the transition honestly. "I left deliberately, because it was time" is more compelling than any polished narrative.
  5. Watch for the pull to undersell. Your "I'm far from being a technical expert" self-deprecation was appropriate inside TELUS where you represented the brand. It's actively harmful now. Your judgment and experience are the product.
